Teaching and Learning English

Stefano has suggested that I look into teaching English online, since I am pretty knowledgeable about English, and because it's flexible in terms of time and location, so I would be able to do it even when I moved back to Brazil. I have thought about it in the past, but initially decided against it because it seemed it would be difficult to teach English since I'm monolingual. But Stefano encouraged me, saying that I could instead focus on advanced learners.

On many occasions I have considered creating materials for advanced learners, but the idea of doing face-to-face calls is quite intimidating. But part of the reason that it's intimidating is likely because I am sick at the moment and it's making me pretty depressed about everthing in general.

So my basic idea at this point is just to look into English teaching online in a way that's low pressure, no commitment. I'm just going to read about it and watch videos, and catalogue the information that I find here. Even if I decide against pursuing this particular path, maybe it will be helpful to someone else who is considering the same path.

Steps for Getting Started
  1. Get a TEFL certification
  2. Decide if you would like to be hired with a company, use an established platform (like Italki), or promote your own services
  3. Start applying for jobs or looking for students, depending on the path you choose. If you're looking for your own students, consider running ads.
  4. You will need some resources: a video platform (ex. zoom), a virtual blackboard, props or assets, and lesson plans.

Terms to Know

TEFL
"Teaching English as a Foreign Language". This is one of the most popular certifications for teaching English online. Many online platforms require you to have this or a similar certification in order to teach English on their platforms.
