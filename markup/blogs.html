Blogs

The idea of a "blog" kind of lost itself in the last few years. Originally, it wasn't anything fancy or crazy, but instead just a way of recording information as it happened. I think there's some kind of value in that.

Yea, I think there's a kind of value in recording your life and your opinions, and sharing that with others, come what may. I think compared to "social" media it can be a bit more isolated, especially when you are starting, but that's just how it is I think. Like, I have started so many blogs, but I always felt they needed to focus on one specific topic, so I kept abandoning them, inevitably, when my interests shifted. I'm hoping that this will be different.

And you might say, "Ghast, what would make this different?" and I think that's a fair question, but I also think that the answer is pretty straightforward. I read and make notes basically every day. I have done this for years now, both on paper and digitally. I write half-finished essays, even when I have no intention to publish them. I make long diary entries that are never even intended to be seen by the public. So, I feel that this will be different, because it will be just me taking all of my notes. When I have an interest, I add my notes to it. When my interest fades, I move to something else.

I think for me, when something is low pressure, when it's casual and no one else is looking at it, that's the point where it becomes something I can comfortably move in and out of. I think I probably write about one million words in an average year, mainly notes and non-fiction essays, but sometimes also fiction as well. Most of it, honestly, is half-finished, but I think I should just embrace that at this point, really. That's just part of "me".
